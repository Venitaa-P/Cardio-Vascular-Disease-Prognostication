Heart Disease Overview:
Heart disease refers to various types of heart conditions. The most common type is coronary artery disease, which can lead to heart attack.

Risk Factors:
- High blood pressure
- High cholesterol levels
- Smoking and tobacco use
- Diabetes
- Obesity and overweight
- Physical inactivity
- Unhealthy diet
- Excessive alcohol consumption
- Family history of heart disease
- Age (risk increases with age)
- Stress

Common Symptoms:
- Chest pain or discomfort (angina)
- Shortness of breath
- Pain in neck, jaw, throat, upper abdomen or back
- Pain, numbness, weakness or coldness in legs or arms
- Fatigue
- Irregular heartbeats
- Dizziness or lightheadedness

Prevention Strategies:
- Don't smoke or use tobacco
- Exercise regularly (at least 30 minutes most days)
- Maintain healthy weight
- Eat heart-healthy diet (fruits, vegetables, whole grains)
- Limit saturated fats, trans fats, sodium and sugar
- Manage stress effectively
- Get quality sleep (7-9 hours per night)
- Regular health screenings
- Limit alcohol consumption
- Control blood pressure and cholesterol

Treatment Options:
- Lifestyle changes
- Medications (statins, beta-blockers, aspirin)
- Medical procedures (angioplasty, stent placement)
- Surgery (bypass surgery)
- Cardiac rehabilitation

Emergency Signs (Seek Immediate Help):
- Chest pain or pressure
- Severe shortness of breath
- Fainting or severe lightheadedness
- Rapid or irregular heartbeat with shortness of breath
- Severe sweating with chest discomfort

Healthy Lifestyle Tips:
- Choose nutrient-rich foods over processed foods
- Include omega-3 fatty acids (fish, nuts)
- Practice portion control
- Stay hydrated with water
- Limit processed and fast foods
- Read nutrition labels
- Cook at home more often
- Find physical activities you enjoy
- Take breaks from sitting
- Practice relaxation techniques
